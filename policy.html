Privacy Policy

We respect your individual privacy and treat your Personal Data in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA). This Data Protection Policy (“Policy”) explains to you how we deal with the information that we collect from you, in particular, the purposes for which we collect, use and disclose your Personal Data. This Policy applies to all Personal Data processed by DANDELIONS through its websites, domains, applications (Apps) or other platforms (collectively “Platforms")

  1. Personal Data

    "Personal Data" refers to any data, whether true or not, about an individual who can be identified (a) from that data; or (b) from that data and other information to which we have or are likely to have access. Examples of Personal Data which may be collected by us include the following:

    • Identity and Contact Data such as your name, address, telephone number, date of birth, country of nationality or residence, national identification number, employment history, educational background, personal background, health data, professional qualifications and job title and function;
    • Physical Data,such as photographs, surveillance and video recordings of you;
    • Financial and Payment Data,such as your bank account information and other related billing or payment information which you have provided us in any forms you may have submitted to us, or in other forms of interaction with you;
    • Business Information,such as information provided in the course of our contractual relationship with you or your organisation or otherwise voluntarily provided by you or your organisation.
  2. Collection of Data

    We collect your Personal Data when you interact on our Platforms or visit our premises.

  3. Consent of Collection of Personal Data

    When you provide Gardens by the Bay any Personal Data relating to a third party (such as information on your dependent, spouse, children and/or parents), you represent and warrant that you are authorised to do so and/or the consent of that third party has been obtained for the collection, use and disclosure of the Personal Data by us for any of the purposes listed in this Privacy Policy.

  4. Disclosure and Transfer of Personal Data

    We will take reasonable steps to secure your Personal Data against any unauthorised access, improper use or disclosure and ensure that access to your Personal data is limited to persons whom we reasonably believe requires access for effective delivery of our services to you.

  5. Usage of Cookies

    Generally, we use cookies to simplify the logging on process for registered users, to help ensure the security and authenticity of registered users, to provide the mechanisms for online shopping and to enable traffic monitoring. Non-registered users are sent anonymous cookies to keep track of their browsing patterns and estimate our audience size. This allows us to compile statistics to understand how users use our Websites and help us improve the structure, services and features of our Websites. In addition, any visitor who has clicked on a website advertisement on another site that links to our Websites will be sent a session cookie (which are temporary cookies that remain in the cookie file until you leave) that will allow us to analyse advertising effectiveness and manage our relationship with affiliate sites. You will not be personally identified in this exercise. You do not need to allow your browser to accept cookies in order to browse our Websites or access our services. However, you must be cookies-enabled if you wish to do online transaction or access any area reserved for registered users.
